        <h3 class="yck-h3">Key Programmes</h3>
        <details name="keyprog">
            <summary class="yck-h4">Effective Readers Programme</summary>
            <p>
                Secondary 4 Express and Secondary 5 Normal (Academic) students
                contribute to the school community by serving as
                <b>Reading Mentors</b> to Secondary 1 and 2 Normal Technical students.
                Every Tuesday and Thursday from 7:15 to 7:45 a.m., these mentors are
                paired with their junior buddies to support their reading development.
            </p>
            <p>
                The Reading Mentors model fluent reading and help explain challenging
                vocabulary. The Normal Technical students then take turns reading to
                their mentors, allowing them to practise reading aloud, improve fluency,
                and enhance word recognition skills in a supportive and encouraging
                environment.
            </p>
        </details>
        <details name="keyprog">
            <summary class="yck-h4">Reflect and Share</summary>
            <p>
                Each week, two to three students from every class, level, and stream are
                selected to speak on an issue introduced by the teacher, following a
                class reading of selected articles from subscribed magazines. These
                students will share their personal opinions and present reasoned
                arguments either in support of or against the issue.
            </p>
            <p>
                Following the presentations, all students will reflect and write down
                their own perspectives on the topic. This process encourages every
                student to consider different viewpoints and develop critical thinking
                skills through thoughtful reflection.
            </p>
        </details>
        <details name="keyprog">
            <summary class="yck-h4">News in Brief</summary>
            <p>
                Each week, three students are selected to share on current affairs
                happening in the world,
                <abbr title="Association of Southeast Asian Nations">ASEAN</abbr> and in
                Singapore. They do their research using
                <a class="text-link" rel="noopener noreferrer nofollow" target="_blank" href="https://www.straitstimes.com/">ST online</a>,
                decide on the news to feature, summarise the news and present to the
                class. They need to reflect on the impact the news has on Singapore and
                our community.
            </p>
            <p>
                Through this, students are made aware of the happenings around them,
                have the platform to build confidence in speaking and to think more
                deeply on issues that affect us.
            </p>
        </details>
        <details name="keyprog">
            <summary class="yck-h4">Extensive Writing</summary>
            <p>
                Students engage in a variety of writing activities designed to cultivate
                regular writing habits and enhance their skills. These include
                <b>Daily Writing</b> exercises lasting 2, 5, or 10 minutes, where they
                reflect on what they have read or heard, plan their essays, or activate
                prior knowledge before tackling a comprehension passage.
            </p>
            <p>
                In addition, students participate in <b>Weekly</b> or
                <b>Fortnightly Writing</b>, choosing topics of personal interest to
                encourage sustained and enjoyable writing practice. They also undertake
                <b>On-demand Writing</b>, where they respond to teacher-assigned prompts
                within a 50-minute time frame.
            </p>
            <p>
                Together, these writing tasks provide students with consistent and
                purposeful opportunities to develop confidence, fluency, and versatility
                in their writing.
            </p>
        </details>
